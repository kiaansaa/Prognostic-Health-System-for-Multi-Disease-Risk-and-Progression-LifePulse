Information About the Diseases We Predict

Learn about the diseases and their symptoms that our system can predict.

Diabetes

Diabetes occurs when blood glucose levels are too high. Insulin is used to regulate glucose intake, and without it, glucose stays in the blood.

Symptoms
  • Urinating often
  • Extreme thirst
  • Fatigue
  • Blurry vision

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer forms in the cells of the breasts and is one of the most common cancers worldwide, especially in women.

Symptoms
  • Breast lump or thickening
  • Changes in size, shape, or appearance
  • Dimpling of the skin
  • Redness or pitting of the skin

Chronic Kidney Disease

Chronic kidney disease is the gradual loss of kidney function. It leads to the accumulation of fluid and waste in the body.

Symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Muscle cramps

Liver Disease

Liver diseases include conditions where the liver is damaged, often causing swelling, jaundice, and easy bruising.

Malaria

Malaria is a mosquito-borne disease that causes fever, chills, and in some cases, can be fatal if not treated properly.

Symptoms
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Nausea

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection that causes inflammation of the air sacs in the lungs, leading to difficulty breathing and coughing.

Symptoms
  • Cough with mucus
  • Fever and chills
  • Shortness of breath
